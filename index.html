I am a computational cognitive scientist in Chris Summerfield's lab at Oxford. My current research looks at how humans and artificial agents are able to flexibly generalize to novel environments with the help of heuristics as simple building blocks. We compare human learning to machine learning with the help of neural network approaches.

Last year, I worked jointly with Lloyds Banking Group and Warwick Business School on a project involving vast amounts of financial big data – individual banking transactions by over 20 million customers - to uncover behavioural patterns with reinforcement learning and information theory approaches, that can be predictive of future behaviour in a longitudinal approach. Some media coverage here. Before this, my PhD was done at UCL in Bradley Love's lab on reconciling heuristic and Bayesian approaches to decision making. From 2016-2017, I was Teaching Fellow on the the MSc Cognitive and Decision Sciences course.






Before returning to academia, I founded the company Brainpool.ai - an AI platform that connects academics in machine learning and data science with corporations for part-time projects. Brainpool delivers solutions to private/public sectors such as healthcare, tech, finance or government. If you are an academic interested to join the world-wide network, please head to our website.
